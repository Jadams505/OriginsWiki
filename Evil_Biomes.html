Evil biomes

If you are looking for the official page of vanilla evil biomes, click [link here | | https://terraria.wiki.gg/wiki/Evil_biomes].

An evil biome is a naturally spreading, hazardous [link biome | | Biomes.html] that has generally negative effects on entities inside or around it. The four currently-implemented evil biomes are [link the Corruption | | https://terraria.wiki.gg/wiki/The_Corruption], [link the Crimson | | https://terraria.wiki.gg/wiki/The_Crimson], the [link Defiled | | Defiled_Wastelands.html] and the [link Riven | | Riven_Hive.html], of which only one will always spawn upon world generation. There is a fifth planned evil biome known as the [link Ashen | | Ashen_Factory.html] that will be released in a later content update of Origins in the future. With AltLibrary support in Origins, the player will be able to pick from any of the four randomly or any one by choice.

It is possible to create artificial versions of each evil biome by seeding 200-block areas with the other evil biome of choice. While this is traditionally done with materials brought over from another world, there is another option: if the [link Dryad | | https://terraria.wiki.gg/wiki/Dryad] is housed in a [link Graveyard | | https://terraria.wiki.gg/wiki/Graveyard] during Hardmode, she will sell grass seeds from the other evil biome's. This allows for the obtaining of alternate materials offered from the other evil biomes.

While there are some consistencies with the generation of each evil biome, they still feature relatively unique world generation comprised of infected [link Ebonstone | | https://terraria.wiki.gg/wiki/Ebonstone_Block], [link Crimstone | | https://terraria.wiki.gg/wiki/Crimstone_Block], [link Defiled Stone] or [link Spug Flesh]. However, the biome can overlap other biomes, providing corrupted versions of the [link Forest | | https://terraria.wiki.gg/wiki/Forest], [link Desert | | https://terraria.wiki.gg/wiki/Desert], [link Snow biome | | https://terraria.wiki.gg/wiki/Snow_biome], [link Jungle | | https://terraria.wiki.gg/wiki/Jungle], or [link Ocean | | https://terraria.wiki.gg/wiki/Ocean]. This can be evidenced by grass an trees that take on different appearances in a similar appearance to their respective biomes.

While in [link pre-Hardmode | | https://terraria.wiki.gg/wiki/Pre-Hardmode], the only biome capable of spreading the evil biomes is infected surface Forest, via infected grassy blocks.

Spread

All of the evil biomes [link spread | | https://terraria.wiki.gg/wiki/Biome_spread] except for the [link Ashen | | Ashen_Factory.html], claiming adjacent tiles to their own versions. Their capabilities of spread in pre-Hardmode are limited, but are capable of spreading faster and more aggressively once the [link Wall of Flesh | | https://terraria.wiki.gg/wiki/Wall_of_Flesh] has been defeated. Defeating [link Plantera | | https://terraria.wiki.gg/wiki/Plantera] for the first time reduces the overall spread of each by 1/2 (50%).

Evil Desert and Snow biomes

In Hardmode, the evil biomes can spread through a [link Desert | | https://terraria.wiki.gg/wiki/Desert] or [link Snow biome | | https://terraria.wiki.gg/wiki/Snow_biome] transforming them into their respective evil versions. Both the evil Desert and Snow biomes are fairly similar to the regular evil biome types with the music and enemies being the same, except for some cases like the [link Eutrophic Sea] for instance. The evil Deserts feature their own set of enemies alongside the typical evil enemies as well.

Evil Jungle biomes

Similar to the evil deserts, the evil biomes are able to occupy the Jungle and create their respective evil jungle grasses. This biome will still behave like the normal evil biome except that if a [link Green Solution | | https://terraria.wiki.gg/wiki/Solutions] is used, the tiles will convert back to [link Jungle Grass | | https://terraria.wiki.gg/wiki/Grasses#Jungle_grass].

Purification

In Hardmode, the evil biomes can spread out of control; without player efforts, the evil biomes will together take over the entire world.

In pre-Hardmode, players can use [link Purification Powder | | https://terraria.wiki.gg/wiki/Thrown_Powder] to purify small portions of the Corruption/Crimson, and [link Sunflowers | | https://terraria.wiki.gg/wiki/Sunflower] to block spread of infected grass. However, it is not until Hardmode that the spreading biomes become a major issue.

In Hardmode, a good strategy is to dig 3-tile-wide tunnels around the Corruption/Crimson, containing it. However, near the surface [link thorny bushes | | https://terraria.wiki.gg/wiki/Plants#Thorny_bushes] can spread the Corruption up to 6 tiles, so the tunnels there need to be 6 tiles wide or lined with non-corruptible blocks. Thorny bushes in Ashen, Defiled, or Riven worlds is not a concern, however.

Removing the Corruption or Crimson whole, or reversing its spread in Hardmode, effectively requires usage of the [link Clentaminator | | https://terraria.wiki.gg/wiki/Clentaminator]. This can be purchased for [price 2|0|0|0] from the [link Steampunker | | https://terraria.wiki.gg/wiki/Steampunker], who arrives after at least one [link mechanical boss | | https://terraria.wiki.gg/wiki/Mechanical_bosses] has been defeated. Using the Clentaminator with Green Solution (also purchased from the Steampunker) can purify large sections of the evil biomes. However, most players will want to keep some well-contained pockets of the biome, as the enemies there have unique drops (some of which are recommended to advance in the game).

The evil biomes are also blocked by the presence of the Hallow, and vice versa; even before obtaining the Clentaminator, seeding the Hallow is an alternate strategy to limit the spread of Corruption or Crimson. However, this may mean eventually having to deal with an out of control Hallow in the future.
